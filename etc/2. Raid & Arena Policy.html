[UPDATED]1. Raid Strategy: 150 Osh Efficiency

This policy outlines the tactical delay requirements for high-damage teams to maximize our overall success against the Level 150 Osh boss. The following criteria regarding damage output take precedence over previous team-specific recommendations.

Target Members: This policy applies to all guild members who possess teams capable of consistently recording over 15M damage (achieving this threshold at least twice against the Level 150 Osh boss).

  • Restricted Period: Friday of the Osh Raid week.
  • Required Action: Members meeting these criteria must delay their attacks with these specific teams until the later stages of the Raid (post-Friday).
  • Additional Action: If the total cumulative damage from all your teams exceeds 10M, you are also required to delay any remaining attacks.
  • Exceptions: Attacks during the restricted period are only permitted if coordinated with and approved by the Guild Leadership (Officers) in advance.

[OLD] 2. Arena: Inter-Guild Competition Policy

We aim to balance individual growth with guild harmony. After internal review, the following policy regarding Arena competition has been established:

  • No Prohibition: Attacks between guild members are not prohibited due to various tactical and progression reasons.
  • Priority on Communication: If persistent competition occurs between members, please prioritize direct communication and negotiation to reach a win-win compromise.
  • Systematic Solution: We are exploring the possibility of adjusting Arena reward time zones. I (the Guild Leader) will personally test time zone changes to see if we can de-sync reward windows and minimize direct conflict.

[UPDATED] 2. Arena & Grand Arena: Inter-Guild Competition Policy

We aim to balance individual growth with guild harmony. To minimize friction while allowing for competitive progression, the following rules apply to both Arena and Grand Arena:

  • Competition Scope: These policies now explicitly cover both Arena and Grand Arena environments.
  • Rank-Based Attack Limit: You are prohibited from attacking guild members who are more than two ranks higher than your current position.
    • Example: If you are ranked 15th, you may attack a guild member up to the 13th rank. Attacking a guild member at the 12th rank or higher is prohibited.
  • Frequency Limit: You may not attack the same guild member more than twice per day.
  • Exception for Lower Ranks: These restrictions do not apply when attacking a guild member ranked lower than yourself.
    • Note: Since defensive logs do not display the attacker's rank, it can be unclear who is attacking from a higher or lower position. Members (especially Top 3 rankers) must prioritize direct communication to avoid misunderstandings.
  • Systematic Solution: We continue to encourage adjusting Arena reward time zones to de-sync windows and minimize direct conflict.
    • Reference: How to Change My Time Zone in the Game
    • Reference: A new day in Dominion

    ⚠️ Important Cautions:

    • Time zone changes can only be performed once every 30 days. Once changed, you cannot revert or modify it again for 30 days.
    • This change affects game modes and quest reset times. It may take up to 24 hours for game modes to sync and the reset time to update.

3. Communication & Fair Play

The strength of XoX lies in our ability to coordinate. Please remember:

  • Share your progress and team compositions in the guild chat.
  • Be respectful during Arena rivalries; a compromise is always better than a resource war.
  • Reach out to Generals or the Leader if you have questions regarding the new Raid timing.
